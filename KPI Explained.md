# KPI Definitions for SHELDON Executive Interface

## Overview

This document explains what each KPI means from Dennis Straub's perspective as CEO of AmeriQual Foods. Understanding these metrics helps ensure SHELDON surfaces the right information in the right way.

---

## Operational KPIs

### OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)

**What it is:** A single percentage (0-100%) that measures how well a production line is running compared to its theoretical maximum.

**Why Dennis cares:** This is the #1 metric for manufacturing efficiency. If a line has 85% OEE, that means 15% of potential production is being lost. At scale, even 1-2% improvement can mean hundreds of thousands of dollars.

**Red flag example:** "Line 3 dropped to 62% OEE" → Something is wrong. Machine issues? Staffing? Quality problems?

**Typical targets:**
- World-class: 85%+
- Good: 70-85%
- Needs attention: Below 70%

---

### Schedule Attainment

**What it is:** Did we make what we planned to make today? Expressed as a percentage.

**Why Dennis cares:** This is about keeping promises. AmeriQual makes MREs and food products for military/government contracts. If schedule attainment drops, they risk:
- Late deliveries
- Contract penalties
- Unhappy customers

**Red flag example:** "Only hit 78% of today's schedule" → We're falling behind. Why? Do we need overtime? Are we going to miss a shipment?

**Typical targets:**
- Target: 95%+
- Acceptable: 85-95%
- Problem: Below 85%

---

### Production Rate

**What it is:** Units produced per hour/shift. How fast are we making product?

**Why Dennis cares:** Directly tied to revenue. Higher rate = more product out the door = more sales. Also helps identify if a line is underperforming.

**Red flag example:** "Line 2 running at 400 units/hr vs target of 600" → That line is a bottleneck.

**How to display:** Show actual vs. target, highlight variance.

---

### Downtime

**What it is:** Time when equipment should be running but isn't. Usually broken into:
- **Planned:** Maintenance, changeovers, scheduled breaks
- **Unplanned:** Breakdowns, waiting for materials, quality holds

**Why Dennis cares:** Unplanned downtime is lost money. Every hour a line sits idle is product not being made. He wants to see trends - is downtime getting worse? Which lines? What causes?

**Red flag example:** "12 hours unplanned downtime on Line 1 this week" → Maintenance problem? Need to invest in equipment?

**Key insight:** Planned downtime is expected. Unplanned downtime is the enemy.

---

### Lines Active

**What it is:** How many production lines are currently running right now?

**Why Dennis cares:** Quick pulse check. If he expects 8 lines running and only sees 5, something's wrong. Good for a morning glance or real-time dashboard.

**Red flag example:** "3 of 8 lines down" → Why? Staffing shortage? Maintenance? No orders?

**How to display:** Simple "X of Y lines running" with color coding.

---

### Labor by Line

**What it is:** How many people are working each line, and what's the labor cost per unit produced.

**Why Dennis cares:** Labor is typically the biggest controllable cost in manufacturing. He wants to know:
- Are we overstaffed or understaffed?
- Which lines are most labor-efficient?
- Where can we improve?

**Red flag example:** "Line 4 has 20% higher labor cost per unit than Line 5" → Why the difference? Can we learn from Line 5?

**Key metrics:**
- Headcount per line
- Labor cost per unit
- Labor efficiency % (actual vs. standard)

## Financial KPIs (Future - Sage X3)

### Revenue (Daily)

**What it is:** Total sales dollars for the day/week/month.

**Why Dennis cares:** Top-line growth. Are we hitting targets? How do we compare to last year?

---

### Gross Margin

**What it is:** Revenue minus direct costs (materials, labor), expressed as a percentage.

**Why Dennis cares:** Shows profitability before overhead. If margin is shrinking, costs are rising faster than prices.

**Formula:** (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100

---

### EBITDA

**What it is:** Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization.

**Why Dennis cares:** The standard measure of operational profitability. Used for valuation, debt covenants, and comparing performance over time.

---

### AR Days (Accounts Receivable Days)

**What it is:** Average number of days to collect payment from customers.

**Why Dennis cares:** Cash flow. Higher AR days = money tied up waiting for customers to pay. Can signal collection problems or customer financial issues.

**Red flag:** AR Days increasing over time.

---

### Cash Position

**What it is:** How much cash is available right now?

**Why Dennis cares:** Liquidity. Can we make payroll? Pay suppliers? Take advantage of opportunities?

**Red flag:** Cash below minimum threshold.

---

## Inventory KPIs (Future - Sage X3)

### Total Inventory Value

**What it is:** Dollar value of all inventory (raw materials, work-in-progress, finished goods).

**Why Dennis cares:** Inventory is tied-up capital. Too much = wasted money. Too little = risk of stockouts.

---

### Days on Hand

**What it is:** How many days of inventory do we have based on current usage?

**Why Dennis cares:** Balance between having enough to meet demand and not tying up excess capital.

**Red flag:** Critical items below safety stock days.

---

### Inventory Turns

**What it is:** How many times inventory is sold and replaced per year.

**Why Dennis cares:** Higher turns = more efficient use of capital. Low turns = money sitting on shelves.

**Formula:** Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory

---

## The CEO Dashboard Mindset

Dennis doesn't want to dig through reports. SHELDON should answer three questions:

1. **Morning glance:** "Are we on track today?"
   - OEE, Schedule Attainment, Lines Active

2. **Spot problems fast:** "What needs my attention?"
   - Red flags, downtime spikes, missed targets

3. **Track trends:** "Are we getting better or worse?"
   - Week-over-week, month-over-month comparisons

Surface the exceptions. Highlight what's off-target. Let Dennis focus his attention where it matters.
